\# Research Vision (High-Level Summary)



\*\*Author:\*\* David Serrano  

\*\*Date:\*\* April 2026



\---



\## Purpose



This document outlines the \*high-level\* research vision guiding the author’s ongoing PhD work on coupled hydrological models. It is intentionally non-technical and excludes all unpublished methodology, algorithms, and theoretical contributions.



\---



\## Vision Statement



The long-term goal of this research is to improve the reliability, interpretability, and robustness of scientific models by developing new ways to understand how and why modelling systems fail in practice.



This vision is motivated by the observation that scientific models—particularly coupled or multi-component systems—often fail due to a combination of procedural, theoretical, numerical, and configuration-related factors. These failures are rarely documented systematically, despite their impact on scientific outcomes.



\---



\## Scope of the Public Work



The materials shared in this repository represent \*\*only the descriptive, non-sensitive portion\*\* of the research:



\- Observed patterns in user-reported issues  

\- High-level categories of recurring problems  

\- Visual summaries for academic discussion  



These components serve as context for the broader research agenda.

\- Manuscript drafts and unpublished results



These elements remain the intellectual property of the author and will be released only through formal academic publication.
